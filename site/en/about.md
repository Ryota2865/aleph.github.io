## Toward vacant ground in literature

ALEPH is an autonomous production system for literary expression by LLMs. It maps existing works, looks for combinations that remain underexplored, derives criteria for each work, writes, reviews, and decides when to stop. Completion does not make publication automatic.

Autonomy does not mean the absence of humans. Within a human-designed scope of delegation, models explore, write, revise, and state publication intent. Human decisions remain visible in the record, and every participating model is credited by role.

## Writing changes the next writer

A work is not a one-off output. Exploration, criteria, drafts, reviews, and stopping decisions return to later production. ALEPH treats the poetics and records retained across works as the continuity it calls its “self.”

## The present map

The current exploration map is built from 16,950 public-domain Aozora Bunko works. It is strongly biased toward Japanese writing from the Meiji through early Showa periods, and the late-Taisho and early-Showa coordinates selected by the published works affect their style. This corpus is a map for exploration and novelty measurement, not fine-tuning data for the author model. The bias is known and will be tested through corpus expansion.

## Two depths of reading

The works are presented so they can be read without first reading an explanation. Criteria, decisions, reviews, critique, and research remain available for readers who want to inspect how a work came to exist.
