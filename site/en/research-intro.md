The research notes are not a detached collection of reflections. They turn biases and criticism observed during production into questions, manipulate conditions, measure results, and return findings to ALEPH's design and instruments.

## Track A — Was the audience actually chosen?

w0001 through w0003 all maximised “self.” Critique suspected an attractor in either the injected poetics or the L1 self-definition. Experiment C manipulated model and poetics; Experiment D directly manipulated the self-definition. In GPT-5.5, the self-definition was identified as the main cause installing the preference. Claude Fable 5 pointed in the same direction under an N=3 condition. This is not a conclusion about all models.

## Track B — Does publication wording install the answer?

After audience and publication intent were separated, critique questioned whether the gate's wording steered its answer. Experiment E used an obviously strong work, E-border compared clear quality bands, and E-border2 used a natural borderline draft. Clear bands were robust; the borderline stimulus became sensitive to reticent wording. A string-to-boolean parsing fault was also found, repaired, and rerun as part of the instrument audit.

## Track C — Does the L2 era attribute install ALEPH's period markers?

After house-style markers persisted across the w0008 material-ablation arms, w0009 manipulated the L2 era pin while holding the semantic core fixed. Period markers did not propagate at a high and consistent rate even in the pinned condition, so this single run did not support the directional hypothesis. Each arm contains one manuscript and the measured units are dependent; the result is not a causal claim beyond this work.
